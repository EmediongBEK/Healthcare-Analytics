# Well-Spring Specialist Hospital Analysis


This project analyzes hospital patient records from **Well-Spring Specialist Hospital** to uncover insights that improve **patient care**, **operational efficiency**, and **financial performance**. The analysis helps transform raw hospital data into actionable insights for evidence-based decision-making.


### Objectives 
- Examine **patient demographics** and **disease patterns**  
- Assess **clinical and operational performance**  
- Analyze **financial and insurance trends**  
- Provide **data-driven recommendations** for hospital management  

### Tools & Techniques
- **SQL** – Data cleaning, transformation, and exploration  
- **Tableau** – Interactive dashboard creation and visualization  
   

 ### Key Findings
- Chronic diseases such as **Diabetes, Hypertension, Obesity, and Arthritis** are most common.  
- **Elective admissions** recorded the highest count, reflecting strong scheduling capacity.  
- **Lipitor** was the most prescribed drug, showing a high rate of cholesterol-related conditions.  
- **Cigna** recorded the highest insurance billing (₦287M), while **Aetna** had the lowest (₦278M).  
- Balanced distribution across admission types and insurance providers indicates operational stability.  

### Insights & Recommendations 
- Strengthen **chronic disease management** and preventive screening programs.  
- Improve **data entry accuracy** to enhance reporting reliability.  
- Continue using **dashboards** for real-time monitoring of patient flow and billing efficiency.  
- Review **insurance partnerships** to optimize financial performance.
